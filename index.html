About

I study the intersection of AI and society. My research examines how intelligent systems shape human behavior, cognition, and social organizationâ€”often in ways that contradict conventional assumptions.

My work identifies paradoxes created by AI and digital platforms: how social media can cause the most engaged users to learn the least, how making AI more honest can reduce user trust, and how AI-driven homogenization of content may paradoxically increase collective innovation. I am also developing a theory of Human-AI-AI-Human interaction, examining what happens when people increasingly act through AI agents that interact with each other on our behalf.

Methodologically, I combine digital trace data, survey panels, experiments, formal theory, and agent-based modeling.
